FAQs - Fake News and Misinformation

1. How can fake news impact university students' academic and social lives?

Fake news can spread misinformation that affects students' understanding of key topics and creates social divisions.

2. What strategies can students use to identify credible sources online?

Look for author credentials, cross-check facts with multiple trusted sources, and avoid sensational headlines.

3. How does misinformation spread faster through social media compared to traditional channels?

Social media's algorithms often prioritize sensational content for engagement, which can amplify misinformation.

4. Why is it important to distinguish between misinformation and disinformation?

Misinformation is unintentionally false, whereas disinformation is deliberately misleading, and knowing the intent matters.

5. How do confirmation biases contribute to the spread of fake news?

People are more likely to believe and share news that aligns with their pre-existing beliefs, even if unverified.

6. Can artificial intelligence play a role in combating fake news? If so, how?

AI can detect patterns of misinformation, flag false claims, and promote verified content through advanced algorithms.

7. What role does media literacy play in fighting the spread of misinformation at Meru University?

Media literacy helps students critically evaluate information, empowering them to make informed decisions and share responsibly.

⚠️ CAUTION: Always verify the credibility of information before sharing it. Fake news can have serious consequences! ⚠️
